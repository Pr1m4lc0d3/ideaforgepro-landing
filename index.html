The problem

Founders build first and find out last.

You spend six months building, then go looking for someone to pay for it. That is the wrong order. Most ideas die in the market, not in the code — the code was never the hard part. The hard part is proving somebody will hand you money, and that can be answered in an afternoon.

The seven gates

Every gate separates the bloom from the slag.

Each gate has a graduation test. Fail one and the forge makes you fix it before you go on — because a weak buyer poisons every answer that follows.

01

Buyer

A nameable, reachable person who has already paid for something like this. Not a market — a person.

02

Problem

Painful, frequent, already worked around — in their words, not yours, with a reason to act now rather than next year.

03

Offer & price

The outcome they are buying, at a number they say yes to without a meeting. Defensible against doing nothing.

04

Value artifact

Something real you can hand over before the product exists — and that does the selling while you sleep.

05

Pre-build test

A real dollar or a signature before any code, with the pass line and the kill line written down in advance.

06

Channel

One channel, gone deep, aimed at people already showing intent. Not a list you bought.

07

Builder spec

Only now: the minimum build that delivers the promise. Cold, hard, and ready to hand to whoever writes it.

How it stays honest

It marks what it cannot prove.

Confident AI that invents numbers is worse than no AI at all — especially in a document you are about to send to someone who matters. So this one shows its work.

It sends unsourced claims back

A hard number with nothing behind it — a market size, a percentage, a "research shows" — is caught before it reaches you and returned to the model to hedge, cite, or retract. Survive a second pass and you see it flagged.

It will fail your idea

Gates come back weak or dead with a reason. Flattery here would cost you months, so you won't get any.

It never sees your data

Your idea and your API key stay in your browser and go straight to your provider. No account, nothing on our servers.

It separates proof from guesswork

Every claim carries its support — proved, asserted, or estimated. The marks live inside the document and survive export, print, and re-import.
